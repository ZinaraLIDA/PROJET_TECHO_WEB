Introduction and scientific context of the project, objectives.

A circadian rhythm is a biological rhythm lasting about 24 hours, which has at least one cycle every 24 hours period. It is an internal clock, nestled in the heart of the brain, which imposes the circadian rhythm on the body, like a conductor. All animal and plant species, molds and cyanobacteria have their own internal clock, set at their own pace. The most visible circadian rhythm in plants is the position of the leaves and petals, which straighten or open more or less depending on the time of day. In humans, the circadian rhythm is not innate. It begins to establish itself around the first eight weeks of life and will sit in the hypothalamus. The latter is made up of two suprachiasmatic nuclei each containing about 10,000 neurons which exhibit electrical activity oscillating over about 24 hours. This electrical activity is controlled by the cyclic expression of about 15 genes called clock genes .The suprachiasmatic nuclei then regulate various functions of the body through direct or indirect messages. They directly and indirectly innervate brain regions specializing in different functions.

In humans, several biological processes are known to be under circadian regulation. These include the cycle of cell division and DNA repair, RNA processing and transport, cell metabolism, and immune system function. Notably, several key immune system parameters in the blood show circadian rhythms, which include the number of circulating hematopoietic and immunocompetent cells, as well as hormones, monoamines, and cytokines. In fact, communication between the circadian clock and the immune system appears to be two-way. The circadian clock influences the elements of the immune system in its two branches : the innate branch (e.g. Rhythmic expression of cytokines and rhythmic functions of monocytes and macrophages); and the adaptive branch (e.g. expression and rhythmic counting of T and B lymphocytes) of the immune system and is involved in models of immune diseases (e.g. Hodgkin lymphoma). Interestingly, recent data shows that the molecular clock controls metabolic pathways, including immunometabolism, and that the immune response could alter the rhythmicity of the clock.

Physiological and behavioral mechanisms also follow a circadian rhythm in humans. Among these, we can find : the sleep / wake rhythm; variations in vigilance; body temperature; blood flow; urine and stool production; hair growth; potassium level.

The circadian rhythm is also found in the expression of clock genes. The expression of these genes in an organism changes over time. This development can be sudden or gradual, one-off or regular.

Strictly speaking, circadian rhythms are endogenous, that is, they are generated by the body itself. These work even in the absence of external stimuli, under perfectly constant light and temperature conditions, for weeks or even months. Under constant conditions, these endogenous rhythms make it possible to maintain the 24 hours periodicity. The period of a circadian cycle consists of three parts : a peak; a hollow; and a medium level.

However, there are exogenous rhythms, caused by stimuli, which evolve independently of our biological clock and which do not conform to this 24 hours period. The main causes are : jet lag which forces the body to resynchronize its circadian rhythm with a new nycthemeral rhythm; nocturnal exposure to artificial light (especially during night work), or even to intense light pollution; a diet high in fat, which disrupts sleep by disrupting the circadian rhythm of adiponectin production. Indeed, concerning this last cause, adiponectin, a hormone synthesized in adipose tissue, plays an important role in lipid storage by regulating fat burning via an increase in insulin sensitivity (which promotes fat loss).

Disturbances in the circadian clock are also known to be associated with several types of disease. In humans, several studies have confirmed that circadian desynchronization with the environment is correlated with a higher incidence of several types of cancer. These include non-Hodgkin lymphoma, breast, endometrial, prostate, and colon cancers.

These informations illustrate the important regulatory role of circadian rhythm in humans and thus support the further development of new therapeutic approaches, involving chronotherapy and other time-dependent intervention strategies. As treatment, there may be mentioned, for example, phototherapy and chronopharmacology. Phototherapy is a therapy that relies on exposure to high intensity light for a fixed period of time (usually between 30 minutes and 1 hour), at a particular time which depends on the individual and the disorder. The patient is encouraged to reduce their exposure to light in the evening, and to remove any electronic device with screens from their room at least one hour before the desired bedtime. Chronopharmacology is the study of the effects of drugs according to circadian rhythms, in order to provide optimal treatment to patients and to minimize side effects. This is because drugs can have different results over a 24 hours period, which means that the effects of drugs are influenced by when they are administered during the day. Efficiency varies with circadian changes in pH, temperature, cell cycles, or protein synthesis.

In our study, we are particularly interested in the circadian rhythm of the expression of these "clock genes" and their modifications in a pathological context such as the development of cancer. The project aims to study changes in the circadian rhythm of gene expression in humans in the context of Hodgkin lymphoma. Hodgkin lymphoma is a type of lymphoma (cancer of the lymphatic system) characterized by the presence of large atypical cells, Hodgkin cells (mononuclear) and Reed-Sternberg cells (multinuclear). These cells have been formally established as being of B lymphoid origin, hence the need to study them together. Our study focuses precisely on these two cell lines : the cell line of B lymphoblastoid cells on the first patient; and the Hodgkin lymphoma cell line on the second. Samples were taken every three hours over a 24 hours period so that 9 time points could be analyzed for each cell line.

For data processing, certain technical tools are used : Python and AWK which are programming languages widely used in bioinformatics; Galaxy, an open source web platform for data-intensive biomedical research; R which is an open source language and software environment for statistical and graphical calculations; DAVID which is a database for integrated annotation, visualization and discovery of genes; Nextcloud which is an online content collaboration platform.

Our circadian rhythm study is done in several stages : data collection, Normalization, metacycle analysis and finally the search for genes ontologies on David.

Data collection

To begin our circadian rhythm study, we started by uploading the raw data of both patients from the Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO) data into ArrayExpress (E-MTAB-6400). ArrayExpress is one of the repositories recommended by major scientific journals to archive functional genomics data from microarray and sequencing platforms to support reproducible research. There are public databases. Next, we retrieved the configuration files available on the Affymetrix web page. Not all of these files are needed for normalization. For basic normalization, the .pgf and .clf files or only the .cdf file are required. We also retrieved the Affymetrix annotation file from the same page. The annotation file is a simple two-column tabular file, with the first column containing the names of the probe sets and the corresponding second annotations, it should not contain any column headers. This file is generated from the Affymetrix annotation file.

The DNA chips :

The DNA chip is a technique for measuring the expression of genes. Usually there are wells on a small square glass plate. In each well there is a piece of DNA called a probe. We incorporate radiometric target fragments on this plate. If the target fragment associates with the probe, then there is emission of a fluorescent light, the stronger the light intensity, the stronger the expression of the gene in the original sample. In our chip images, we do not find any artifacts that could compromise the quality of our DNA chip. On our results, we only see the image of the chip with luminous dots.

The scree plot

The Scree plot of the PCA allows us to know according to how many components we will find a significant separation. In this scree plot, we can see that the bend of the curve is at the level of the 3rd component, so we will have a difference according to three components in this PCA.

Principal Component Analysis (PCA):

It is a statistical method that makes it possible to explore so-called multivariate data (data with several variables). Each variable could be considered as a different dimension along which the variation of the data is maximum. In other words, PCA reduces the dimensions of multivariate data to two or three principal components, which can be visualized graphically, losing as little information as possible.

Metacycle

The Metacycle package is mainly used for detecting rhythmic signals from large scale time-series data. In Metacycle there are two functions-meta2d and meta3d for detecting rhythmic signals from time-series data sets. For analyzing time-series data sets with individual information, meta3d is suggested, which takes use of any one of these three methods to analyze time-series data individual by individual and gives out integrated values based on analysis result of each individual. meta3d can be executed with three algorithms, JTK_cycle(JTK), ARS and LS.

JTK_Cycle is a non-parametric algorithm developed. Its purpose is to identify rhythmic components in large, genome-scale data sets and estimate their period length, phase, and amplitude.

ARSER(ARS) is a package for identifying periodic expression profiles in analyzing circadian microarray data and has been released under the GPL.

The Lomb–Scargle(LS) periodogram approach is used to search time series of gene expression to quantify the periodic behavior of every gene represented on the DNA array

To continue our study after normalization from Metacycle we ran meta3d with our normalized data that we put as a parameter as well as our design file that we created from our conditions (gene identifier), patient (1 and 2), cell type and time as we can see :

Conclusion, work organization, difficulties encountered, interest and prospects

On the statistic analysis, the results are low, we have search which genes follow a circadian rhythm in the two cancer patients. Thanks to R project, we collected all the statistics we needed to make a conclusion for the patients.

We can conclude from this analysis that the two patients have some problems in their clock genes expression.

For the first patient, there are only 42 significant genes with a circadian rhythm, it’s very few in a comparison of the number of genes implicated in the circadian rhythm. We find also 3198 genes with an other algorithm we saw thanks to DAVID that all these genes find are implicated in similarities functions. They are implicated in DNA replication, DNA translation, pH regulation, translation regulation, nucleosome regulation, detection of DNA anomaly, extracellular regulations.

For the second patient we saw 7 significant genes in all the algorithms. It’s very minimal. These significant genes are implicated in the nucleosome regulation and in the extracellular regulation. We lost all the genes clocks implicated in the DNA replication and translation, in the pH, the translation, nucleosome regulation.

The cancer affects a lot of clock genes expression. In the case of the cell line of B lymphoblastoid cells (patient 1), this cancer affects on many genes but there is few functions (only 7 molecular functions) that stay with gene having a circadian rhythm.

One the other hand, the patient 2 with Hodgkin lymphoma cell line cancer is more affected than the patient 1. He has just 7 potentially genes having a circadian rhythm and only 2 molecular functions are stayed with genes following a circadian rhythm.

In fact, the B lymphoblastoid line cells are affected on the circadian rhythm but it is less affected than the Hodgkin lymphoma cell line.

We can search more data to make a better analysis including more patients with these types of cancer and also more data for the same patient. We could correlate the two researches and see if the first one was significant or if it was close to the new one or even if the first one had wrong data in comparison to the second research.
